📉 Negative Highlight: Optimization Methods Fail on 7-Day Window

🔍 Key Finding: All optimization methods (E3 fusion, E4 knowledge transfer, E5 learnable weights, E2 node weighting) produce higher sMAPE than the baseline (31.82%) on the 7-day window, indicating they introduce noise rather than improving accuracy.
Reasons for Ineffectiveness:
  • E3 (Granularity Fusion): Tsinghua fine-grained data (30-min → 6-hour) captures only 1-day patterns, which mismatches the weekly patterns needed for 7-day prediction. sMAPE rises to 39.25%.
  • E4 (Knowledge Transfer with Temporal Weights): Simple repetition of the 4-slot SHAP weights over 7 days destroys the weekly variation. sMAPE = 37.93%.
  • E5 (Learnable Temporal Weights): Only 4 learnable parameters cannot model the complex 28-step pattern; sMAPE = 39.62%.
  • E2 (Node Weighting): Global average weights among node types are nearly identical (industrial: 0.00728, commercial: 0.00807, residential: 0.00787), making weighting effectively uniform. sMAPE = 39.25%.
